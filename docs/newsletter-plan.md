# Newsletter Plan — first four issues

*Mark's outline, 16 July 2026. Sent via Buttondown (from news.yourkids.com)
once signups are live; see `next-tasks.md` §6 for the activation state. Voice
per Site Bible §2 — warm, friendly, humble; never "the best", always honest.*

## Issue 1 — Hello, and what this is

- **Overview of yourkids.com** — brief, not comprehensive, warm tone. What the
  site is (carefully sourced articles, guides, free apps), who it's for, and
  the promise: no judgement, no spam, everything free.
- **An ask for new ideas** — invite replies: what would help you most? What
  should we build or write next? (Replies go to the from-address; make sure
  it's a monitored inbox.)

## Issue 2 — The free apps + activity wheel deep dive

- Introduction to the apps launcher (`/tools`) — one line each.
- **Deep dive: the "What shall we do today?" activity wheel**
  (`/tools/activities`) — how it works on phones, the daily pick, inside vs
  outside, age bands.
- **How to save it to your home screen** — step-by-step for **Android**
  (Chrome ⋮ → *Add to home screen*) and **Mac** (Safari: File → *Add to
  Dock*; Chrome: install icon in the address bar). *Suggested addition:
  iPhone too (Safari share → Add to Home Screen) — most parents are on
  phones.*
- **Top 3 articles** — short summaries with links.

## Issue 3 — Baby & Toddler app deep dive

- **Deep dive: the Baby & Toddler app** (`/app`) — the daily Today screen,
  week-by-week guides, feeding modes; worth being explicit that everything
  stays on your device (name + date of birth never leave your phone).
- **Top article** — one, summarised properly.

## Issue 4 — Educational games

- **Introduction to Educational Games** (`/games`) — now live, so this is a
  launch announcement: circle-time games (Buzz!, Odd One Out, Chain Reaction,
  Make the Number), quiz packs, co-operative games for mixed ages, printable
  cards, and What the Word?! for team play across phones. Worth showing the
  classroom angle (projector scoreboard, spelling-list mode) and inviting
  teachers/group leaders to reply with what they'd use next.
- **Summary of key articles.**

## Production notes

- Compose in Buttondown (manual for these four); the automated engine
  `digest` stage (next-tasks §6) can take over the "top articles" section
  later.
- Every issue: one-click unsubscribe (Buttondown adds it), link to the
  privacy policy, and the reply-to ask — building the habit of replies is
  the point of a small list.
- Keep issues short enough to read one-handed while feeding a baby.
