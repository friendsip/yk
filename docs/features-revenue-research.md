# Features & Revenue Research — Source Notes (June 2026)

Companion to `features-revenue-presentation.html`. Three parallel research sweeps; key findings with sources. Figures are as reported by the cited sources.

## Track 1 — What parenting sites are shipping (features)

**The landscape shift**
- ~60% of Google searches end without a click; CTR drops to ~8% under AI Overviews ([The Digital Bloom](https://thedigitalbloom.com/learn/2025-organic-traffic-crisis-analysis-report/), [AdExchanger](https://www.adexchanger.com/publishers/the-ai-search-reckoning-is-dismantling-open-web-traffic-and-publishers-may-never-recover/))
- Mumsnet: traffic declining ([Similarweb](https://www.similarweb.com/website/mumsnet.com/)), but 1.5M words posted daily and ~half of traffic is direct ([journalism.co.uk](https://www.journalism.co.uk/news/how-mumsnet-s-new-ceo-turned-25-years-of-women-s-voices-into-ai-gold/s2/a1282003/)). First UK org to sue OpenAI ([Waterfront Law](https://waterfront.law/mumsnet-becomes-the-first-organisation-to-take-legal-action-against-openai-in-the-uk/))

**Proven feature patterns**
- Age/date-keyed trackers are the universal anchor (BabyCenter, What to Expect — claims reach to 91% of US first-time expecting mums, [CB Insights](https://www.cbinsights.com/company/what-to-expect))
- Huckleberry SweetSpot (predictive nap windows, $59/yr) — the moat for a whole subscription business ([Pregnant Chicken review](https://pregnantchicken.com/huckleberry-worth-upgrading/))
- Name finders still grow: Nameberry ~3.38M monthly visits
- Raising Children Network: best no-login age-stage architecture; development trackers as pure content pages ([raisingchildren.net.au](https://raisingchildren.net.au/)); monthly expert webinars
- UK school-term planner: underserved gap — no major player does it well
- Lovevery: everything keyed to developmental stage; $1B lifetime sales 2025 ([Businesswire](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20260512203069/en/))

**AI features — the winning pattern**
- Good Inside "Gigi": RAG chatbot grounded in Dr Becky's corpus; app ~$25/mo, 100k+ members, $34M/yr revenue ([Fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/02/27/dr-becky-kennedy-good-inside-revenue-leadership-playbook-for-parenting-34-million-a-year-business/), [Fast Company](https://www.fastcompany.com/91305862/dr-becky-good-inside-ai-parenting-app))
- Ask Lovevery: RAG over their own course/Q&A base ([CZI](https://chanzuckerberg.com/newsroom/lovevery-launches-mobile-app-with-resources-for-parents-and-caregivers/))
- Pattern: parent-facing AI grounded in a trusted corpus lands well; child-facing AI attracts backlash
- Novel 2024–26: "mental load" apps (Ohai.ai, Milo — school-calendar parsing); Summer Health (GPT-4 visit notes, [OpenAI case study](https://openai.com/index/summer-health/)); UChicago Chat2Learn

**Community**
- Open SEO forums dying as acquisition; community as retention/destination survives
- Cohorting by birth month (What to Expect) is the most effective community structure
- Scheduled expert Q&A/webinars beat open forums for new entrants (moderation is the cost, not code)

## Track 2 — Revenue models

**Membership**
- Real-world free→paid conversion: 1–3% (not the 5–10% marketing claim) ([Lenny's Newsletter](https://www.lennysnewsletter.com/p/what-is-a-good-free-to-paid-conversion))
- Typical pricing $6–9/mo; Substack takes 10%
- Good Inside: $34M, 100k members ([Fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/02/27/dr-becky-kennedy-good-inside-revenue-leadership-playbook-for-parenting-34-million-a-year-business/)); ParentData: tens of thousands paid at ~$60/yr; all winners are persona-led
- At 1.5% / $8/mo: 5k list → ~$6.5k/yr; 25k → ~$32k/yr; 100k → $130–260k/yr
- Churn risk: parenting is stage-based — subscribers churn when the stage passes

**Newsletter sponsorship — the standout**
- **Family category: $32 revenue per 1,000 subscribers per send — top of all consumer categories** ([Paved benchmarks, Apr 2026](https://www.paved.com/blog/newsletter-sponsorship-rates/))
- beehiiv Ad Network minimum: 2,500 subscribers ([beehiiv guide](https://www.beehiiv.com/blog/newsletter-sponsorship-cost)); Passionfroot 5–15% fees
- Weekly send: 5k subs → ~$8k/yr; 25k → ~$40k/yr; 100k → $150k+/yr

**Affiliate beyond Amazon**
- Family finance is the high-EPC outlier: Ethos $20–55/lead, Ladder $75/lead ([Authority Hacker](https://www.authorityhacker.com/insurance-affiliate/))
- Edtech/boxes: Outschool up to 20% (Awin), KiwiCo $10/sub, Lovevery $15–20/sale, Green Kid Crafts $10 renewing
- Amazon toys 1–3% remains lowest-rate/highest-conversion
- Caveat: review content is the segment hardest hit by AI Overviews

**Display ads**
- Parenting RPMs ~$20–32 (Mediavine), Raptive higher (~$54 reported by one switcher); Journey by Mediavine accepts from 10k sessions
- Sector revenue falling — bloggers earning $3–8k/mo saw it "fall sharply" ([Blog Herald 50-blogger survey](https://blogherald.com/interviews-commentary/i-asked-50-bloggers-if-theyre-still-making-money-in-2026-the-answers-were-brutal/))

**Digital products**
- Big Little Feelings: $99 toddler course, 400,000 course-takers ([Fortune Creator 25](https://fortune.com/ranking/creator-25/2021/big-little-feelings/))
- Etsy printables: $200–2,000/mo typical, top sellers $3–10k/mo at 85–95% margins
- **Twinkl: £98.9M revenue FY2025** — printables-as-subscription at scale ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Twinkl))

**B2B licensing**
- Nemours KidsHealth licenses paediatric content to health systems/payers ([Adobe case study](https://blog.adobe.com/en/publish/2021/08/26/nemours-childrens-health-leading-pediatric-digital-content-globally-through-adobe-experience-manager))
- Buy-side: employer family-benefits platforms (Cleo — 180+ employers; Maven Clinic)
- Plausible early book: 3–5 licensees at £5–25k/yr. Buyers demand editorial provenance — human-reviewed curation is an asset, pure AI generation disqualifying

**Directories**
- Psychology Today: 80,000 therapists × $29.95/mo ≈ $29M/yr — but referrals reportedly drying up under AI search
- Two-sided cold-start is the barrier; childcare vetting is a liability risk

## Track 3 — Games, AI engagement, compliance

**Browser games as audience drivers**
- NYT Games: 11.1bn plays 2024, ~14.5M daily Wordle players, 1M+ Games-only subs; play+read subscribers retain much better ([Digiday](https://digiday.com/media/the-next-level-for-us-the-new-york-times-eyes-longer-play-sessions-for-games-in-subscription-drive/))
- **NYT family plans (Sept 2025): $30/mo All Access Family, $10/mo Games-only family** — separate Wordle streaks per member; drove Q3 2025 subscriber jump ([Nieman Lab](https://www.niemanlab.org/2025/09/the-new-york-times-launches-a-family-subscription-with-separate-wordles-for-everyone/))
- PBS Kids: ~40M games played monthly ([PBS 2025 Fact Sheet](https://www.pbs.org/about/about-pbs/blogs/news/pbs-2025-fact-sheet/)); BBC Bitesize used by ~47% of UK schoolchildren
- Kids/family daily-puzzle niche nearly empty: Spellie (indie "Wordle for young spellers") is the only direct comparable

**AI story generation**
- Wonderbly (template personalisation, human text): 10M+ books sold, acquired by Penguin Random House 2025 ([Graphite Capital](https://www.graphitecapital.com/news/graphite-capitals-portfolio-company-wonderbly-announces-record-book-sales/))
- Parent attitudes (Nov 2025): AI images acceptable only with human-authored text + expert review ([phys.org](https://phys.org/news/2025-11-parents-kids-ai-generated-images.html))
- Character.AI banned under-18s from open-ended chat (Nov 2025) under lawsuit/FTC pressure — child-facing AI chat is toxic

**Printables**
- Twinkl proves the ceiling; mid-size parenting site reports 25k subscribers from a free printable library; zero data-protection exposure

**Compliance**
- UK Children's Code applies to anything "likely to be accessed" by under-18s: DPIA, high-privacy defaults, no nudge mechanics; fines to £17.5M/4% ([ICO](https://ico.org.uk/for-organisations/uk-gdpr-guidance-and-resources/childrens-information/childrens-code-guidance-and-resources/age-appropriate-design-a-code-of-practice-for-online-services/))
- COPPA amended rule: full compliance by 22 April 2026 ([FTC](https://www.ftc.gov/news-events/news/press-releases/2025/01/ftc-finalizes-changes-childrens-privacy-rule-limiting-companies-ability-monetize-kids-data))
- Minimal-burden configuration: no child accounts, no ads, no profiling, localStorage-only progress, parent-held subscription — matches our planned model; DPIA still required

**Gamification — approved vs condemned**
- Anti-patterns: Prodigy Math (FTC complaint — 16 upsell ads in 19 min of play); individual child streaks with loss-aversion (Duolingo-for-kids criticism)
- Brand-aligned patterns: Khan Academy Kids (no ads/paywalls/data), Pok Pok ("no rules, points, or timers"), Sago Mini (kidSAFE-certified)
- Family-safe streak alternative: household-level "family streak", no individual child pressure, no loss penalty
