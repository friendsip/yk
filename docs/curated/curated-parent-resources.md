# Curated Links: Parent Resources

*Regularly updated links to essential reading, official guidance, and trusted external resources.*

---

## Child Mental Health

### YoungMinds: Understanding CAMHS
**youngminds.org.uk/find-help/for-parents/parents-guide-to-support-a-z/parents-guide-to-camhs**

The clearest parent-facing guide to how Child and Adolescent Mental Health Services work in England, what to expect from a referral, and what to do if your child is refused. Includes guidance on what the different CAMHS tiers mean in practice — something professionals often don't explain.

*Tags: Health*

---

### Anna Freud Centre: Mental Health in Schools Guide
**annafreud.org/schools-and-colleges**

The Anna Freud Centre publishes excellent, evidence-based resources for schools and parents on children's mental health. Their "Mental Health in Schools" guide is particularly useful for parents who want to understand what support schools should be providing and how to advocate for it.

*Tags: Health, Education*

---

### NICE Guidelines on Children's Mental Health — Plain English Summary
**nice.org.uk/guidance/cg28** *(Anxiety: recognition and referral in children and young people)*

The NICE guidelines are the formal clinical standards against which NHS services are assessed. They're written for professionals, but knowing what they say can be useful when you're in conversation with your GP or CAMHS about your child's care. For anxiety specifically, CG28 is the relevant guidance.

*Tags: Health*

---

## SEND and Education

### IPSEA: Free Legal Advice on SEND
**ipsea.org.uk**

If you are navigating the SEND system in England — EHCPs, assessments, school placements, appeals — IPSEA (the Independent Provider of Special Education Advice) is the essential resource. They offer free legal advice, template letters, and factsheets that are written in plain English and are legally accurate.

*Tags: Education, Special Needs*

---

### Contact: Supporting Disabled Children and Their Families
**contact.org.uk**

Contact is a UK charity specifically for families of disabled children. Their website covers everything from benefits and finance to education, healthcare, and family life. Their helpline staff are knowledgeable and accessible. If you're in the early stages of a diagnosis or navigating services for the first time, this is a good first port of call.

*Tags: Special Needs, Family Life*

---

### Ofsted: SEND: Old Issues, New Issues, Next Steps (2021 Report)
**assets.publishing.service.gov.uk** *(search: Ofsted SEND old issues new issues)*

One of the most important recent official documents on the state of SEND provision in England. Written accessibly and with specific attention to the gap between what families are entitled to and what they receive. Essential context for understanding why the system is in its current state.

*Tags: Education, Special Needs*

---

## Online Safety

### Internet Matters: Age-by-Age Online Safety Guide
**internetmatters.org/parental-controls/age-by-age-guide**

Internet Matters is arguably the UK's most practically useful online safety resource for parents. Their age-specific guides cover what children are likely to be doing online at different stages, which risks are most relevant, and step-by-step instructions for parental controls on every major device and platform. Updated regularly.

*Tags: Safety*

---

### UK Safer Internet Centre: Reporting and Support
**saferinternet.org.uk**

The UK Safer Internet Centre runs Safer Internet Day each February and maintains a helpline for professionals working with children online. For parents, their resources include guidance on what to do if your child has encountered harmful content, been contacted by a stranger, or experienced cyberbullying.

*Tags: Safety*

---

### NSPCC Net Aware: Parents' Guide to Social Media
**net-aware.nspcc.org.uk**

Parent-written, regularly updated guides to the social media platforms and apps children are most likely to be using — including ones that have emerged recently. Covers the basics of each platform, the main risks, and what you can do. More accessible and more current than most government guidance.

*Tags: Safety*

---

## Bereavement

### Winston's Wish: Supporting Bereaved Children
**winstonswish.org** | Helpline: 08088 020 021

The UK's leading childhood bereavement charity. Winston's Wish provides direct support for bereaved children and their families, resources for schools, and a helpline for parents and professionals who are supporting a grieving child. Their website has age-specific guidance that is both practically and emotionally informed.

*Tags: Health, Family Life*

---

### Child Bereavement UK
**childbereavementuk.org** | Helpline: 0800 02 888 40

Alongside Winston's Wish, Child Bereavement UK is one of the two main specialist UK charities in this area. Their resources cover a wide range of bereavement types including suicide loss, which often requires specific support. Training for schools and professionals is also available.

*Tags: Health, Family Life*

---

## Family Support

### Family Lives: Parenting Advice and Helpline
**familylives.org.uk** | Helpline: 0808 800 2222

Family Lives offers free support and advice for parents across a wide range of topics — from toddler behaviour to teenage conflict to relationship difficulties affecting children. Their helpline is free and available evenings. Less specialist than some of the other resources listed here, but accessible and genuinely helpful for general parenting challenges.

*Tags: Family Life*

---

### Sibs: Supporting Siblings of Disabled Children
**sibs.org.uk**

The only UK charity specifically focused on the needs of siblings of disabled children and adults. Their resources cover how to support a sibling at different ages, the particular challenges siblings face, and how to access support. Highly recommended for families in which one child has significant needs.

*Tags: Special Needs, Family Life*

---

## Useful Government Resources

### Find Your Local SENDIAS Service
**kids.org.uk/sendias**

Every local authority in England is required to provide a free, impartial Special Educational Needs and Disabilities Information, Advice and Support Service (SENDIAS). These services can help you understand your rights, navigate the EHCP process, and prepare for meetings or appeals. Find your local service through this link.

*Tags: Education, Special Needs*

---

### DfE: The SEND Code of Practice (2015)
**assets.publishing.service.gov.uk** *(search: SEND Code of Practice 2015)*

The legal framework for SEND in England. The full document is long, but Part 9 (EHC Needs Assessments and Plans) and the relevant local authority sections are essential reading if you are navigating an EHCP assessment or appeal. IPSEA's website annotates key sections for parents.

*Tags: Education, Special Needs*
